ABOUT

My name is Hitomi Nakamura. I am a graduate student at the University of Minnesota in the Master of Geographic Information Science (GIS) program. I currently hold a Bachelor of Arts Degree from the University of Minnesota, Duluth in GIS with a minor in Geography.
My hometown is Fukushima, Japan where I experienced the Great East Japan Earthquake in 2011. This incident taught me the importance of preparing for natural disasters and effective urban planning. My goal when I started learning GIS was to support communities by exploring ways to mitigate risk, improve disaster resilience and enhance economic development.
As I pursued GIS, I became interested in both front and back-end web development and database management, and utilizing them for urban planning field.

Dual-Axis Map

In my cartography class, I made a dual-axis map to show the population density decrease from 2010 to 2014 in Iwaki City, Fukushima, Japan. I used georeferencing and edit tool to create the Iwaki City's district polygons. Then, I created and exported both the population density map and the population percentage change map individually using ArcMap. Finally, overlaying and designing the map was done by Adobe Illustrator.
