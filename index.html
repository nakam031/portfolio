ABOUT

My name is Hitomi Nakamura. I graduated from University of Minnesota in the Master of Geographic Information Science (MGIS) program in May 2019, with Minor in Management of Technology. I also hold a Bachelor of Arts Degree from the University of Minnesota, Duluth in GIS with a minor in Geography.
My hometown is Fukushima, Japan where I experienced the Great East Japan Earthquake in 2011. This incident taught me the importance of preparing for natural disasters and effective urban planning. My goal when I started learning GIS was to support communities by exploring ways to mitigate risk, improve disaster resilience and enhance economic development.
As I pursued GIS, I became interested in both front and back-end web development and database management, and creating web application for wider audiences.

Custom Toolbox with Python & ArcPy

I created a custom tool that can do multiple summary statistics with multiple selections of case fields. Since conventional Measuring Geographic Distributions toolset allows only one case field, I decided to customize my tool to calculate with selections of multiple case fields. This tool minimized the process of getting multiple summary statistics since it can have multiple outputs at one custom toolbox. Visit the ArcGIS Online page to view/download the toolbox.
