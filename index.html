ABOUT

My name is Hitomi. I am a graduate student at the University of Minnesota in the Master of Geographic Information Science (GIS) program. I currently hold a Bachelor of Arts Degree from the University of Minnesota, Duluth in GIS with a minor in Geography.
My hometown is Fukushima, Japan where I experienced the Great East Japan Earthquake in 2011. This incident taught me the importance of preparing for natural disasters and effective urban planning. My goal when I started learning GIS was to support communities by exploring ways to mitigate risk, improve disaster resilience and enhance economic development.
As I pursued GIS, I became interested in web development, urban planning and remote sensing. Web GIS has a capability to serve up-to-date information to a large number of people. It is also useful for both natural disaster response and urban planning coordination.
